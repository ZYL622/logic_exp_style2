  • Totally no idea about how to start?
    • First, you can set up an initial framework, which includes the given information and the final derivation.

    \[ \begin{array}{ll} 1.\large|&A \rightarrow (C \rightarrow B) & ~\textup{given}\\ \hline 2.\large|&C& ~\textup{given}\\ \hline ~ ~~~~\large|& ~\textup{.}\\ ~~~~~\large|& ~\textup{.}\\ ~~~~~\large|& ~\textup{.}\\ \hline ~. \large|& A \rightarrow B& \textup{...}\\ \hline \end{array} \]


  • Get stucked(or no idea) after writing down the initial framework?
    • From the final derivation, it is evident that B follows if A is given. However, A is not given. Hence, to arrive at the final conclusion, the assumption of A is necessary. Besides, at the last line, $I_{\rightarrow}$ should be applied.

    \[ \begin{array}{ll} 1.\large|&A \rightarrow (C \rightarrow B) & ~\textup{given}\\ \hline 2.\large|&C& ~\textup{given}\\ \hline 3.~~~~\large|&A & ~\textup{assume}\\ \hline ~~~~~~\large|&\textup{...}\\ ~~~~~~\large|&\textup{...}\\ \hline 6. \large|& A \rightarrow B& \textup{$I_{\rightarrow}(3,...)$}\\ \hline \end{array} \]


  • Unable to go on after correctly finishing line 3(which is in Hint 2)?
    • To move on, it's essential to have a clear objective. Given the task of deriving B with A as a given condition, the second-to-last line should indeed B.

    Besides, according to the first three lines, only lines 1 and 3 can be applied by the rules that you have learned.

    \[ \begin{array}{ll} 1.\large|&A \rightarrow (C \rightarrow B) & ~\textup{given}\\ \hline 2.\large|&C& ~\textup{given}\\ \hline 3.~~~~\large|&A & ~\textup{assume}\\ \hline 4.~~~~\large|&C \rightarrow B& E_{\rightarrow}(1,3)\\ \hline 5.~~~~\large|&B& ~\textup{...}\\ \hline 6. \large|& A \rightarrow B& I_{\rightarrow}(3,5)\\ \hline \end{array} \]
