  • Totally no idea about the starting?
    • First, you can set up a framework, which includes the given information and the final derivation.

    $ \begin{array}{ll} \hline 1. \big| X & \textup{given}\\ \hline 2. \big| Y & \textup{given}\\ \hline 3. \big| X\rightarrow(Y \rightarrow Z) & \textup{given}\\ \hline 4. ~~~~~\big| \textup{...}\\ \hline 5. \big| Z & \textup{...} \\ \hline \end{array} $


  • Get stucked after writing down the framework?
    • Read the $E_{\rightarrow}$ rule!
    For example, based on given information of $X$ and $X \rightarrow (Y \rightarrow Z)$, after applying $E_{\rightarrow}$ rule, we can derive $(Y \rightarrow Z)$.
